## Draft Message

Team - Q3 Roadmap Planning Guidance

We've talked about this in various conversations over the past few weeks, but I want to put it in writing so we're all on the same page as we finalize Q3 plans.

We're currently spending roughly 75% of our capacity on technical work (KTLO, maintenance, modernization, tech debt) and 25% on commercial roadmap work. There is a good amount of understanding and appreciation for why that is needed.

We all want to spend less time on technical debt and more time creating customer value. But before we can start shifting that balance, we need to get to a better state technically and operationally.

We have two must-do initiatives (Red Mythos and Disaster Recovery), but by far the biggest unlock for us is Autonomous Teams. Getting to a state where each team can plan, sprint, build, test, deploy, and operate independently. This doesn't mean achieving a picture-perfect microservice architecture. As a first step, we just need to get to a place where no ***teams*** are locked together by the monolithic codebase, hard dependencies, or shared deployment pipelines.

With that said, as we go through the Q3 planning, the expectation is that the planned work breaks down as follows:
- 25% commercial roadmap
- 75% technical work, which should directly contribute to one of the following initiatives (the "Big 3"):
  1. Red Mythos / Vulnerabilities 
  2. Disaster Recovery
  3. Autonomous Teams / Breaking the Monolith (decoupling, independent deployments, domain ownership)

There will be exceptions for various reasons, but my request is that we keep them to a minimum, and discuss each such exception. I want to be very intentional about them.

I have added a column to the Q3 - 2026 Roadmap Planning spreadsheet. The request is that any line item that has **Roadmap Category** (column M) of ***Technical***  also has a **Technical Initiative** filled in (column N).

Questions, concerns or major issues with this approach? Let's talk!
