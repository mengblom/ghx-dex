# Engineering Manager Expectations for AI - Mandatory

### AI Fluency Expectation for Engineering Managers

- Leaders must become personally fluent with AI tools
- Tools should become integral to daily work, not just team directives
- Apply AI lens to all activities:

- Better communication
- Improved meeting leadership
- Team progress tracking
- Personal example: Email clarity significantly improved using AI

### Team Training Responsibility

- Managers actively responsible for team AI learning
- Integration required in all meetings and one-on-ones
- Must know each team member’s current AI skill level
- Provide ready resources and identify help opportunities
- Company building resource wiki page for managers
- Cannot delegate to external training (Team Jarvis) alone

### Adoption and Impact Metrics Ownership

- Target: 80% of direct reports actively using AI tools by end of month
- Manager responsibility to:

- Track who is/isn’t using AI effectively
- Remove adoption blockers
- Measure concrete improvements from AI adoption
- Impact examples vary by team:

- Increased throughput
- Reduced story completion time (month+ stories completed in 4 days)
- Must compare against historical data for meaningful metrics

### Monthly Team Reporting

- Email monthly digest to director and Team Jarvis
- Include both wins and struggles with AI adoption
- Helps spread successful practices across organization
- Serves as team PR for AI commitment
- Suggested approach: Keep running markdown file, use AI to compile monthly email

### AI-Native Hiring Standards

- AI fluency assessment required in every interview
- Apply to all roles across organization
- Evaluate actual AI knowledge vs. ability to fake knowledge using AI
- Consider live demonstrations (screen sharing Claude usage)
- Look for hunger to learn AI if not already proficient

### Next Steps

- Wiki pages with Anthropic courses and certification links to be shared
- Anthropic courses offer completion certificates for tracking
- Managers encouraged to share relevant videos, courses, articles
- Support available for developing team-specific impact metrics
