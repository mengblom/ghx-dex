# Architecture Strategy and Exchange Resiliency Discussion with Anan Bawa

## Context

First deep conversation with [[Anan_Bawa]], new Product Chief of Staff. Discussed challenges with CJ's asks around proving business value for foundational engineering work, upcoming July 3 architecture offsite, and strategic direction.

## Key Themes

### 1. Anan's Role & Mandate

- **Product Chief of Staff** reporting to Steve Jackson and Marlon
- Brought in to:
  - Improve say-do ratios
  - Maximize ThinkTail/Kickdrum value
  - Ensure lasting R&D operating system after consultants leave
- Digging deeper into engineering than originally planned

### 2. The Business Value Tension

**My struggle:** CJ wants me to prove business value for foundational changes (autonomous teams, engineering managers) that I view as "laws of physics" - they shouldn't need proof.

**Anan's take:** 
- Understands my perspective
- But recognizes organizational context: capacity has been historically opaque, driving need for value articulation
- Suggests we don't need to be pedantic about it - can tie to broader business drivers like exchange stability/uptime
- Most of what I'm doing serves that end

**His pushback areas:**
- People equating uptime with disaster recovery (not the same)
- Excluding API gateway from analysis without understanding impact
- Vague terminology without specific definitions

### 3. The Monolith Problem (Clarified)

I articulated that "the monolith" isn't just the codebase - it's:
- Monolithic team structure (now addressed with 4 directors, 12 managers)
- **Monolithic ways of working** (biggest problem)
- **Monolithic deployment pipelines**
- **Monolithic infrastructure footprint**

Anan agreed this is foundational work that has to be done before anything else can move faster.

### 4. Cardinal Health Context

The HA/DR timeline pressure is driven by Cardinal Health **contractual obligations** - not true DR or general uptime improvements. We need to be laser-focused on meeting minimum requirements, separate from broader resiliency work.

### 5. Architecture Offsite (July 3)

**Current state:**
- Placeholder on my calendar, no briefing
- Focused on ontology & orchestration
- I'm an attendee, not prepped to present anything

**Anan's concerns:**
- Doesn't feel like proper ownership exists
- Struggling to find anyone who can whiteboard current architecture
- Wants to understand "as-is" before discussing "to-be"
- Will push on daily standup (CJ, Var, Steve, consultants) to clarify:
  - What are we trying to accomplish?
  - Where does my ownership fit?
  - What's expected by when?

**Daniel Milburn** identified as best person to whiteboard current architecture (17 years at GHX, knows it all).

### 6. What I Should Do (Anan's Guidance)

**Continue architecting and articulating:**
- Finish research (commitments, code, what's been done)
- Form opinions on what needs to happen
- Present clear priority list with proper sequencing
- Show what has to go together
- This could become "Exchange Resiliency" or whatever we call it

**Framework:**
- Tie to business drivers (exchange stability, feature velocity)
- This is foundational - nothing else can move faster without it
- Should be the model for how we do this anywhere in the codebase
- May become "surge investment" if needed

**Philosophy:**
- "What has to be true" first principles
- Balance target architecture with proper sequencing
- Slot commercial objectives appropriately

### 7. Terminology Issues

Common terms used ambiguously:
- "Tech debt"
- "Exchange resiliency"
- "The monolith"

Need more color/correction to ensure shared understanding.

### 8. Next Steps

**Anan:**
- Get architecture walkthrough from Daniel Milburn
- Push for clarity on July 3 offsite expectations (starting Monday standup)
- Ensure my ownership and timeline are explicit

**Me:**
- Continue developing architectural vision and priority roadmap
- Prepare for July 3 with CJ and Kickdrum
