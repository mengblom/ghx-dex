# Objective 1: Pivot to a World Class Product-Centric Engineering Organization

*This objective is about moving my engineering organization toward a
more modern, product-centric way of working. Teams should have clear
ownership of what they build and run, be structured and staffed to
operate independently, and work within a consistent agile framework that
supports focus and accountability. The intent is to create an
environment where teams can make decisions locally, prioritize
effectively, and move forward without unnecessary dependencies or
coordination overhead.*

*We will also begin a deliberate Reverse Conway Maneuver---shaping team
boundaries and ownership to match the architecture we want over time.
This sets the foundation for evolving Exchange incrementally, including
breaking apart the monolith in a thoughtful way. The outcome we are
driving toward is greater agility: teams that can deploy more
frequently, deliver value in smaller and more meaningful increments,
respond faster to change, and continuously improve the product without
relying on large, risky releases.*

*A key part of this objective is enabling teams to own their full build
and release cycle and to ship code and deliver features autonomously. By
shaping team boundaries and ownership to match the architecture we want
over time, we set the conditions for teams to deploy on their own
cadence rather than being constrained by shared release processes or
monolithic dependencies. This Reverse Conway Maneuver establishes the
foundation for incrementally breaking apart the monolith, increasing
deployment frequency, and delivering value to customers more nimbly and
often.*

- KR1:\
  Define and publish a **target team topology and ownership model**,
  aligned to the future target architecture, covering **100% of
  engineering teams and systems**.

- KR2:\
  Ensure **100% of teams have all critical roles defined and assigned**
  (engineering management, technical leadership, product partnership) to
  operate autonomously within their ownership boundaries.

- KR3:\
  Fill 100% of open roles prioritized for Q1, with hiring priorities
  explicitly aligned to the target team topology.

- KR4:\
  Define, socialize, and baseline an **Agile Maturity Model** across
  **100% of teams**, measuring adherence to agreed agile principles and
  engineering best practices.

- KR5: Ensure **100% of engineers are aware of and have access to
  GHX-approved AI development tools**, and establish a **baseline
  approach for measuring adoption and impact**.

# Objective 2: Delight Stakeholders with Better Product/Value Delivery

*This objective is about tightening the partnership between Engineering
and Product and improving how work flows from idea to delivery. By
applying agile best practices to intake, prioritization, roadmapping,
and execution, we aim to focus engineering effort on the highest-value
work and reduce friction, churn, and surprise along the way.*

*I also want us to improve transparency and confidence in our
prioritization and planning. We, stakeholders, and executive leadership
should have a clear, shared view of what the teams are working on,
what's planned, and how progress is tracking.*

*Getting to autonomous teams that operate independently is an important
part of this journey, especially as it relates to our ability to ship
faster and more frequently.*

- KR1:\
  Establish a **single, shared Product--Engineering roadmap view for the
  Exchange**, reviewed monthly, covering all active initiatives and
  major dependencies.

- KR2:\
  Ensure **100% of engineering work (planned and unplanned)** is visible
  and tracked, eliminating undocumented or "shadow" work

- KR3:\
  Deliver a **service-oriented architecture proof of concept** by
  rebuilding **one Exchange component** as a standalone, cloud-native
  service in lower environments, demonstrating:

  - Clear ownership

  - Independent deployability

  - Operational readiness standards
