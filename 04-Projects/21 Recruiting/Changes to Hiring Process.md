All,

We have a lot of hiring to do over the next few months - over 40 engineering roles across our US and India locations. To handle this volume effectively, we're making some tweaks to our hiring process.

The objectives here are as follows:
- Move faster on great candidates. Top talent has multiple options. We need to evaluate quickly and make timely decisions.
- Reduce overhead for our friends in Talent Acquisition. A streamlined, repeatable process makes their job easier and more efficient.
- Minimize disruption for interviewers. The process should be sustainable for everyone participating in interviews.
## What's Changing

- **Interview Format: Pairs, Not Panels** – Going forward, interviews will be conducted by two interviewers rather than larger panels:
  - One lead interviewer and one shadow/support interviewer
  - The pair decides how to run the interview—traditional lead/shadow, divide-and-conquer, alternating questions, whatever works best for you

- **Three-Phase Structure** – Think of our hiring process in three distinct phases:
  - **Phase 1: TA Screening** – Initial resume review and recruiter phone screen
  - **Phase 2: Hiring Manager Screening** – Technical conversation with the hiring manager to assess baseline and team fit
  - **Phase 3: Interviews** – Once a candidate passes the HM screen, TA will schedule all interviews, ideally across 1-2 calendar days. The standard interview loop includes:
    - **Coding Interview** – Software engineering fundamentals, technical problem-solving
    - **System Design Interview** – Architecture, scalability, tradeoffs
    - **Behavioral/Team Fit** – Collaboration, communication, culture alignment
  - **Additional Interviews**
    - For Staff and Principal Engineers, we'll add a 4th interview:
      - **Architecture & Leadership** – Strategic thinking, technical influence, mentorship
    - For Engineering Managers, we'll add a different 4th interview:
      - **Leadership & Management** – People management, team building, organizational skills
  - We (Engineering) will provide TA with designated interviewers for each interview type to make scheduling efficient. If you are not participating in interviews currently, but want to, please raise your hand.

- **Weekly Hiring Sync** – We'll hold a weekly meeting with hiring managers, interviewers, and TA to:
  - Review pipeline and hiring funnel metrics
  - Discuss borderline candidates or conflicting feedback
  - Make offer decisions for non-obvious cases
  - Calibrate on what good looks like and provide feedback to screeners

## Guidance for Interviewers

In addition to the process changes, I wanted to give a little guidance on what to think about when you are participating in the interview process.

- **Before the Interview**
  - **Accept unless you have a legitimate conflict.** Hiring is a team effort. If you must decline, help TA out by suggesting someone you know is available.
  - **Come prepared:**
    - Know the role and job description the candidate is interviewing for
    - Review the candidate's resume ahead of time
    - Plan your questions and identify areas to probe deeper
    - Coordinate with your pair interviewer on how you'll divide responsibilities
  - **Be ready to sell GHX.** Remember, interviewing is a two-way street:
    - We're making real impact in healthcare—simplifying supply chains so providers can focus on patient care, with 1.3M+ connections and $2.2B in annual savings for the industry
      - https://www.ghx.com/en/about/our-mission/#our-vision
    - We're financially stable with resources to invest in engineering
    - We're building a modern, world-class software development organization for the long term
    - We operate as true product teams with real autonomy and agency
    - We're leaning hard into AI-native development, with tools and resources readily available

- **After the Interview**
  - **Submit feedback promptly.** This is critical:
    - Great candidates move fast—we need to make decisions quickly
    - Your impressions are freshest immediately after the interview
    - **Don't coordinate with other interviewers beforehand.** We want your independent assessment, not group consensus

- **Evaluating Candidates** – Our goal is to build high-performing, autonomous product teams with great culture. Keep these principles in mind:
  - **Do not treat the Job Description as a must-have checklist.** The JD is a guide, not a rigid scorecard. Focus on the candidate's overall fit, potential, and ability to effectively do the role after some onboarding.
  - **Optimize for aptitude and potential, not perfect credentials.** We care more about learning ability and trajectory than exact years of experience or perfect skill matches. Don't be afraid to hire someone at the bottom of the band or even one level down if they demonstrate strong fundamentals and growth potential.
  - **Look for T-shaped engineers.** We want people with deep expertise in one or two areas, but enough breadth to collaborate across the stack and contribute beyond their core specialty.
  - **AI experience matters.** Look for candidates who have hands-on experience with AI-assisted development—or at minimum, demonstrated interest (through personal projects etc.) and opinions on how to leverage AI tooling effectively.
  - **Modern SDLC experience.** Candidates should be familiar with agile/scrum methodologies, CI/CD practices, and frequent deployment cadences.
  - **Leaders with relevant architecture experience.** For senior ICs and managers, look for experience with distributed, cloud-native, microservices architectures—including the operational patterns that make them work (messaging/queuing, observability, deployment strategies, etc.).
  - **Team fit is crucial.** We're building for the long haul. Culture fit and the ability to contribute positively to team dynamics are just as important as technical chops.
  - **Look for product thinking.** We want engineers who care about the problem space, the customer, and the end product—not just the code. Validate this by asking about their current domain and gauge their depth of understanding of the company they currently work for.
