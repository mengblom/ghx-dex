# Rituparna Ghosh - Engineering Manager Interview Summary

**Position:** Engineering Manager - Exchange Platform
**Date:** 2026-03-12
**Interviewers:** Marten, Daniel
**Duration:** 60 minutes

---

## Overall Assessment

Rituparna demonstrated solid hands-on engineering management experience with microservices modernization and team leadership. She showed practical knowledge of cloud infrastructure, observability, and people management (coaching, PIPs, performance differentiation). However, **significant concerns emerged** around architectural ownership, product collaboration depth, and agile philosophy alignment.

**Key Highlights:**
- **Hands-on leadership** - Personally owned 5 microservices while managing 3 teams during modernization
- **Microservices modernization** - Greenfield migration to 50+ microservices on GCP
- **Performance management** - Clear differentiation between high/low performers, PIP experience
- **Observability setup** - Distributed tracing with Dynatrace, per-service dashboards, alerting
- **Team development** - Training focus for less experienced engineers, AI tool adoption

**Key Concerns:**
- **Heavy reliance on architect** - Multiple mentions of "working with my architect" for tech decisions
- **Shallow product collaboration** - Could not articulate product partnership beyond feature requests
- **Agile misalignment** - "Sprint demos are more for product owners" signals misunderstanding of agile ceremonies
- **Communication** - Somewhat shaky, possibly nervousness, but concerning for leadership role

## Critical Concerns

### 1. **Architectural Dependency**
- Mentioned "working with my architect" **multiple times**
- In this role, she needs to **BE the architect**, not rely on one
- Raises questions about independent architectural decision-making capability
- May struggle in role requiring strong technical vision ownership

### 2. **Product Collaboration Depth**
- When asked about Product, gave **one-sentence answer**: "I work closely with product for new feature requests"
- **Could not elaborate** despite follow-up prompting
- This is a critical partnership for an EM role
- Suggests surface-level product engagement

### 3. **Agile Philosophy Misalignment**
- Statement: **"Sprint demos are more for the product owners"**
- This contradicts industry-standard agile practice (demos for stakeholders, team transparency, feedback)
- Raises concerns about understanding of agile ceremonies and their purpose
- May indicate command-and-control vs. collaborative mindset

### 4. **Communication Clarity**
- Communication was "shaky" throughout interview
- While possibly nervousness, concerning for leadership role requiring:
  - Clear stakeholder communication
  - Team guidance
  - Executive updates

While Rituparna has solid hands-on engineering management experience and demonstrates good people management fundamentals, **critical gaps emerged** in architectural ownership, product partnership depth, and agile philosophy. The repeated reliance on an architect is particularly concerning for a role requiring independent technical leadership.

### Confidence Level
**Medium-High** - Interview provided clear signal on key concerns. The architectural dependency pattern and shallow product collaboration responses were consistent themes, not isolated incidents.
