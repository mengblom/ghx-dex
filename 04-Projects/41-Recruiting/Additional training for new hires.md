# Message from Ramesh

Subject: Exchange hiring - expectations from the Recruitment team

Curtis & Marten,

During my Sync with Tasha on India hiring,  I highlighted that our current compensation ranges are significantly below market for top-tier talent. Tasha clarified that GHX's positioning does not support top-market compensation and that we should align with more conservative budget constraints.

While she noted that exceptions are possible, the Engineering leaders should be prepared to hire candidates who may require additional training to bridge skill gaps. 

This does not align with our talent acquisition goals and standards we discussed when you were in India. My concern is that this "hire-to-train" approach will lower our performance standards and extend our timelines to realize the tech revamp goals that we have set for ourselves. 

Ultimately, “Product mindset” will be a casualty in lowering the bar.

# Reponse
This phrase “additional training” is being used quite often around our hiring process, so I want to clarify how I want us to think about this.

**“Additional training” is _not_ a strategy to hire underqualified candidates at lower cost and then train them up to the role.** We should assume normal onboarding and stack ramp for every hire—but the candidate still needs the **core fundamentals for the role and level** on day one. The only exception is when we find someone with unusually strong potential but a real gap; in that case we should **hire them at the appropriate lower level** (with level-appropriate expectations), not try to “train” them into a higher-level role.

Some made-up examples of what would be OK vs. not OK, just to illustrate the point:

- ✅ Strong backend engineer who hasn’t used our specific Java framework (e.g., Spring Boot) → **_OK (learn the framework)._**
- ❌ Legacy/Fortran-only engineer with no modern backend/service experience → **_not OK to assume they’ll “learn Java + modern services” through training._**
- ✅ AWS-experienced engineer who hasn’t used a specific service like SQS → **_OK (tooling gap, not fundamentals)._**
- ❌ For a frontend role: backend-only engineer with zero UI experience → **_not OK to hire into a frontend role assuming they’ll pick up React/TS/CSS as “training.”_**

Bottom line: we should be flexible on **tools and specific technologies**, but not on **role fundamentals and level expectations**. We can be flexible without lowering the bar or turning hiring into a retraining program.

Marten
