7. **You introduced CI/CD pipelines across 3 products at Zebra - how did you drive that initiative?**

8. **At Fannie Mae you led design of cloud native applications with event-driven architecture improving performance 30% - walk me through that initiative?**
    - Had a legacy monolith
    - Problems: deployment time huge, lots of dependencies across teams, components could not be scaled individually, problems with team velocity and deployment frequency, time to resolve issues
    - Approach:
        - Domain-driven design: break monolith into services by domain, not technical layers
        - Identified areas that could be separated
        - Started with 1 functional area that could be easily moved out (proof of concept)
        - Validated approach, then picked "one of the critical areas" that could be easily separated
        - Shifted traffic slowly to new service vs. shifting all at once
    - Challenges:
        - Other services/apps still had dependencies on monolith as each service came out
        - Created abstraction layer between new microservices and monolith
        - "Instead of polluting the new microservice with all the request response format of the existing monolith"
        - Abstraction layer stayed until corresponding components moved out
        - Downstream consumers not being ready
    - Multiple teams involved: ingestion team, scoring team, ML team - each transitioning to microservices
    - Resistance from team:
        - "Definitely there was some resistance and some open expression sometimes within the team"
        - "This is a push coming from leadership" (VP/EVP level)
        - Some people continued, some understood importance, some saw benefits once journey started
        - Saw deployment times reducing, quick time to fix issues
    - Balancing with ongoing work:
        - Had to support existing infrastructure + new architecture
        - Main concern: not adding too many features to legacy (would be burden to transfer)
        - "Had to keep for certain things and take only the ones we can meaningfully achieve based on customer impact"
        - Product team friction: wanted speed for customer issues vs. time on architecture
        - Had to explain benefits to product team
