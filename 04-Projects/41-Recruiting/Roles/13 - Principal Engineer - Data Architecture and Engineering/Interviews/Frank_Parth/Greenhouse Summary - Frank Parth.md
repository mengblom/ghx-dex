## Summary

**Fundamental role mismatch.** Frank is a healthcare integration/interface engineer (HL7/FHIR/EDI specialist), not a data architect. When asked about core expertise, he self-described as "medical interface engineer and data repository." His database experience is using NoSQL as storage backends within integration platforms, not architecting data systems or driving technology decisions.

**Pros:** Exceptional healthcare domain knowledge (14 years HL7/FHIR/EDI), strong credentials (BS/MS CS, MS Data Science), hands-on and persistent, programming proficiency (Java/C#/Python). **Cons:** Passive role in technology decisions (DocumentDB choice: "our team decided to go with it" with no architectural rationale), shallow performance optimization depth (only mentioned indexing, no query tuning/execution plans), limited architectural breadth (implementing within platforms vs. designing data models), communication gaps (couldn't articulate trade-offs when probed), title/scope mismatch (Senior Principal title but described senior IC work).

**Recommendation: Do Not Advance.** Frank would excel as Senior Integration Engineer or Healthcare Interoperability Architect at an EHR vendor or HIE. This Principal Data Architect role requires evaluating database technologies with technical depth, designing data models for complex business domains, and guiding application teams on data decisions. Frank's passive role in technology selection and inability to articulate architectural trade-offs is disqualifying for a Principal position requiring teams to trust his guidance.
