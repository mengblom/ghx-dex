**Interview confirmed the mismatch:**

- Current enablement is training business users on reporting tools (Power BI sessions)
- Job search motivation: "Almost into maintenance mode instead of build mode" - seeking another **platform-building** greenfield opportunity

**Assessment:**

Matt's resume is accurate and his interview was honest - there's no credibility issue. The problem is a **role mismatch**:

- **Matt's sweet spot:** Building centralized BI platforms (data warehouses, data marts, ETL pipelines) that serve reporting needs across an organization. He excels at modernizing legacy data infrastructure and creating analytics capabilities.

- **This role requires:** Embedding within application teams, reviewing their code/architecture, providing real-time guidance on data modeling within applications, helping teams make independent data decisions.

These are **different jobs** requiring different collaboration patterns:
- **BI Platform Engineer** (Matt): Centralizes data, builds platforms others consume, trains users on tools
- **Application-Embedded Data Architect** (This role): Decentralizes expertise, reviews code, enables team autonomy

Matt would likely struggle in this role not due to lack of technical depth, but because:
1. His career has been building platforms, not embedding with teams
2. His enablement approach is vertical (train business users) vs. horizontal (peer collaboration with engineers)  
3. His job satisfaction comes from greenfield platform builds ("build mode") rather than ongoing architectural guidance
4. Best Buy experience was "waterfall, no agile" - limited exposure to modern cross-functional team patterns

**Conclusion:** Strong resume for a BI Platform Engineering or Data Warehouse Architect role. Wrong resume for an application-embedded Principal Data Architect position. The mismatch is clear and career change at this stage (20+ years) would be significant.
