## The Core Argument (WHAT vs HOW)

**Sales reviews WHAT teams deliver (revenue), not HOW they work (calls made).**
**Engineering should follow the same principle.**

### The Distinction
- **WHAT (Outcomes):** Revenue, features shipped, system reliability → Review publicly ✅
- **HOW (Process):** Calls made, deployment frequency, lead time → Teams own ❌

### Gaming Asymmetry
- **Gaming WHAT = Achieving goal:** Can't fake revenue ✅
- **Gaming HOW = Bypassing goal:** Can fake deployment frequency without value ❌

### Why It Matters
When you optimize for WHAT metrics, you actually improve outcomes. When you optimize for HOW metrics, you just manage optics (no impact, or negative impact on value).

### Blameless Postmortems Prove It Works
- Outcome accountability: Incident happened, customer impact (WHAT) ✅
- Process protection: Who made mistake, what they were doing (HOW) ❌

### Psychological Safety Connection
- Process metrics require safety to report honestly
- Leadership review destroys that safety through implicit performance pressure
- This was the key insight that landed with CJ

## Key Lessons Learned

### What Worked
1. Raising concerns directly - CJ appreciated the challenge
2. Psychological safety framing - Resonated more than gaming concerns
3. WHAT vs HOW framework - Clear, principled boundary
4. Acknowledging assumptions - "I jumped to conclusions" built trust
5. "Try it and see" approach - Better than preemptive fight

### What Didn't Work
1. Vague research claims - "Lots of research" without specifics prompted challenge
2. Assuming worst case - Amazon comparison without evidence
3. Leading with gaming - Came across as "don't trust me" resistance

### For Next Time
- Lead with psychological safety, not gaming concerns
- Cite specific research when claiming backing
- Don't assume intent without evidence
- Focus on "how to make it work" vs "why it won't"
