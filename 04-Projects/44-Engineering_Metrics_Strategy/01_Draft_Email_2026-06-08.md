# Draft Email to CJ - Metrics Review Concerns

**Subject:** Concerns About Team Performance Metrics Review Approach

---

CJ,

Following up on the discussion in the TLT meeting about reviewing metrics. Here are my thoughts:
1. I'm fully aligned on the core intent - we don't use metrics enough and need to level up. 
2. System health metrics, like Arshad presented (incidents, application performance, SLOs etc.), are completely appropriate for "public" leadership review.
3. My concerns are specifically about reviewing **individual team performance metrics (DORA metrics)** in detail at the leadership level:
	1. DORA metrics are designed for teams to improve their own delivery practices, not for performance reviews. When these metrics become targets reviewed by senior leadership, they stop being useful - people start managing to the metric instead of managing the underlying work. Goodhart's Law: "When a measure becomes a target, it ceases to be a good measure."
	2. When engineers know senior leadership is watching their metrics, behavior changes:
		- Move a work item back to "In Progress" after a code review issue? Or just leave it and fix it quietly?
		- Report an incident that reveals a problem? Or handle it quietly?
		- Be honest about deployment failures? Or find ways to not count them?
	3. As Ramesh put it: this creates a "tendency to manage optics rather than manage team health." The data degrades, consciously or unconsciously.
4. I do think it is helpful to discuss how teams are working with these metrics - how they have automated the collection, insights and actions they have lead to etc. But not dissect the numbers for an individual team "publicly".
5. This doesn't mean hiding metrics - I actually want them publicly available. It's the forum of picking them apart with senior leadership present that's counterproductive. Regardless of how sincerely we frame the intent, it will feel like going to the principal's office and people will adjust their behavior accordingly.

In addition, I have some ***temporary*** concerns with our DORA metrics (these will resolve over time):
- **Teams aren't stable yet:** DORA metrics are only valid when teams are in stable states - well-defined ownership, fully staffed, established processes. At least in Exchange and Automation, almost no team meets these criteria right now. We're reshaping ownership, actively hiring, and building up teams.
- **Manual collection is counterproductive:** We need automation from our systems (Jira, GitHub, deployment tools). Manual collection is time consuming (especially packaging them up nicely for a potential public review) and not very accurate. 

**What I recommend:**
1. Limit public leadership review to system health metrics (incidents, SLOs, application performance)
2. If we want to discuss team performance metrics in these larger forums, keep it more general, more like brown bag discussions where teams share "here's how we used metrics to find and fix a problem"
3. Once automated and teams are stable, keep metrics publicly visible but don't create a stage for picking apart individual team performance

Thoughts? Happy to discuss further.

Marten
