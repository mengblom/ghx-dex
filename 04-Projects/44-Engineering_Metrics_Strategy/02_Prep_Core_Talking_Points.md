# DORA Metrics Discussion - Talking Points

## Core Position
**Sales reviews WHAT teams deliver (revenue), not HOW they work (calls made).**
**Engineering should follow the same principle.**

---

## The Distinction: WHAT vs HOW

**WHAT (Outcomes)**
- Sales: Revenue, deals closed, customer retention
- Finance: EBITDA, accuracy, audit results  
- Engineering: Features shipped, system reliability, incidents
- **→ Review in leadership forums ✅**

**HOW (Process)**
- Sales: Calls made, emails sent, CRM updates
- Finance: Journal entries, time to close, hours worked
- Engineering: Deployment frequency, lead time, MTTR
- **→ Teams own for self-improvement ❌**

---

## Why This Matters: Gaming Asymmetry

**Gaming WHAT = Achieving the Goal**
- Can't fake revenue → Gaming "close more deals" means closing deals ✅
- Can't fake EBITDA → Gaming profitability means improving profitability ✅
- **Path to gaming goes through achieving goal**

**Gaming HOW = Bypassing the Goal**
- Can fake deployment frequency → Deploy trivial changes, no value ❌
- Can fake lead time → Cut code review corners, quality degrades ❌
- **Path to gaming bypasses goal entirely**

**This asymmetry is everything.** Optimizing for WHAT improves outcomes. Optimizing for HOW creates theater.

---

## Postmortems: Yes, There IS Accountability

**We ARE accountable for WHAT:**
- ✅ Incident occurred, customer impact, system failures
- ✅ Leadership reviews outcomes and holds org accountable

**We're NOT blamed for HOW:**
- ❌ Who made mistake, what they were doing, why they did it
- ❌ "Blameless" separates outcome accountability from process blame

**Apply same to DORA:** Review WHAT (features, reliability), protect HOW (deployment mechanics)

---

## Where We Stop (Slippery Slope Response)

**Same boundary in every function:**
- Sales: Review revenue ✅, not calls made ❌
- Finance: Review EBITDA ✅, not journal entries ❌  
- Engineering: Review features shipped ✅, not deployment frequency ❌

**Not arbitrary - based on gaming asymmetry and incentive alignment.**

---

## Why "Spin the Wheel" Makes It Worse

- Creates ambient surveillance ("any week you could be called on")
- Constant pressure to keep metrics looking good
- Teams optimize for "presentation ready" vs actual improvement
- **Artifact-as-forcing-function destroying honest measurement**

---

## Key One-Liners

**Core distinction:**
"Sales reviews WHAT - revenue. DORA measures HOW - deployment mechanics. When you review HOW in leadership forums, teams optimize for the metric instead of the outcome."

**Incentive difference:**
"Gaming a sales metric means closing deals. Gaming deployment frequency means shipping trivial changes. One achieves the goal, one bypasses it."

**Slippery slope:**
"This isn't engineering being special. Same principle that makes sales and finance work: Accountable for WHAT, autonomy for HOW."

**Postmortems:**
"Blameless postmortems prove it works - outcome accountability (incident happened) + process protection (who/how). Apply same to DORA."

**Psychological safety:**
"Process metrics require safety to be honest. Leadership review destroys that safety. You can't have both."

---

## The Close

**"Hold teams accountable for outcomes (WHAT), empower them to optimize process (HOW)."**

**This is how we manage sales and finance successfully. Apply it to engineering.**
