# Meeting Notes: Engineering Metrics Discussion with CJ

**Date:** June 9, 2026
**Participants:** Marten Engblom, CJ
**Duration:** 15 minutes

---

## Key Outcomes

### CJ's Intent (Clarified)
- **NOT about comparing teams** - Marten jumped to conclusions based on Amazon "spin the wheel" pattern
- **IS about learning** - Similar spirit to blameless postmortems, but for metrics
- **Wants to embody company values** - "Measure what matters" and "Trust in God, everyone else brings data"
- **Committed to psychological safety** - Acknowledged this is critical and will develop over time

### Agreement Reached
- **Try it and see** - Psychological safety will be demonstrated through behavior over multiple meetings
- **Learning meeting, not performance review** - CJ wants to find the right phrasing (like "blameless postmortem" for incidents)
- **Focus on learning, not criticism** - "Here's my data, here's what I learned, here's what I'm improving"
- **Will address off-point comments** - If 20% of discussion is critical/unhelpful, will address and reduce

### Outstanding Concerns
- **Automation gap** - Metrics are largely manual effort right now, not dashboard-ready
- **Cost of preparation** - Takes hours to prepare, then only 1 team presents
- **Six-year problem** - CJ: "We've talked about this for six years, we're out of time, especially with AI"

---

## Key Quotes from CJ

**On intent:**
> "My commitment is that there is a valuable discussion we can drive with metrics."

**On comparison:**
> "We are not comparing teams. I don't know why people jump to these conclusions."

**On learning from Amazon:**
> "Amazon does it and you can be a gentle baron, or you can actually use it as a learning exercise. I'm trying to figure out how do we do it positively and without being jerks?"

**On psychological safety:**
> "It's about how we lead it together. Same discomfort as PIR review - it's about how you run it."

**On measuring:**
> "Every other function has validated themselves to run a metrics-driven shop. Ironically engineering, which is so analytical, runs into these debates."

**On urgency:**
> "We've talked about this for six years and we've run out of time. Specifically because AI is destroying all this. You've got to have measurements in place."

---

## Key Points from Marten

**On WHAT vs HOW:**
> "We don't measure how many phone calls [sales] make or how many rows in spreadsheets. We don't measure how they do their work, we measure the outcome."

**On executive-level review:**
> "Where it becomes dangerous is when that is reviewed at an executive level."

**On incidents vs process:**
> "There's definitely accountability when something went wrong. But we focus on what went wrong in the process, not what an individual or team did wrong."

**On psychological safety (the key insight that landed):**
> "What's needed is not really about the levels or executive review - it's about the psychological safety existing in that forum. The first meeting it's not going to be there, probably not the second, but over time Curtis and especially you would demonstrate that's the case."
