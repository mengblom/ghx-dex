# Organizational Maturity Observations

*Last Updated: 2026-03-02*

## Hiring and Talent Assessment Maturity

### Current State (March 2026)

**Organizational Immaturity:**
- Not very mature in how we look at roles and talent
- Quite inexperienced at interviewing and judging candidates
- Treating JDs as checklists rather than guides
- Even TA (Talent Acquisition) is too stuck on wanting precise criteria for what defines a good candidate

**The Problem:**
- Binary thinking: candidate either perfectly matches checklist or doesn't
- Missing the nuance of potential, adjacent skills, and growth capacity
- Inability to assess candidates holistically
- Over-reliance on exact match criteria

### What Good Looks Like

**Better Approach to JDs:**
- Job descriptions are guides, not checklists
- Look for capability patterns, not exact keyword matches
- Assess potential and learning ability, not just current skills
- Consider adjacent experiences and transferable skills

**Interviewing Maturity:**
- Ask probing questions about how candidates think
- Explore problem-solving approaches
- Understand their learning and growth trajectory
- Assess cultural and team fit beyond technical skills

**TA Partnership:**
- TA should help translate intent, not enforce rigid criteria
- Flexible interpretation of requirements based on market reality
- Partner on finding right candidates, not just matching keywords
- Understand the "why" behind the role requirements

**Why This Matters:**
- Cloud-native architectures require teams to manage their own infrastructure
- Can't have centralized infrastructure team own everything

**Implications for Hiring:**

## Recruiting Values and Principles

*Added: 2026-04-17 from April 2026 recruiting planning*

### Core Hiring Philosophy

**Optimize for Potential, Not Perfection**

Rather than seeking the "perfect" candidate who checks every box, prioritize candidates with:
- Strong foundational skills and demonstrated learning ability
- Growth trajectory and potential to develop
- Adjacent experiences that transfer to the role
- Problem-solving mindset over specific technology expertise

**Why This Matters:**
- Perfect-match candidates are rare and expensive
- High-potential candidates grow into the role and often exceed expectations
- Focusing on potential expands the talent pool significantly
- Creates a learning culture rather than a "hire experts only" culture

### Key Attributes to Prioritize

**1. T-Shaped Candidates**
- **Depth:** Strong expertise in at least one area (the vertical bar of the T)
- **Breadth:** Ability to work across multiple domains (the horizontal bar)
- Can dive deep when needed, but also collaborate broadly
- Generalist mindset with specialist skills

**2. AI Experience and Mindset**
- Direct experience using AI tools in development workflows
- Understands how to leverage AI for productivity
- Curious about AI capabilities and limitations
- Aligns with our AI Native Development vision
- Can help the team adopt and improve AI practices

**3. Growth and Learning Orientation**
- Demonstrates continuous learning
- Adapts to new technologies and approaches
- Shows evidence of skill development over time
- Comfortable with ambiguity and change
- Can learn domain knowledge on the job

### What This Means in Practice

**During Screening:**
- Don't eliminate candidates for missing one or two requirements
- Look for patterns of learning and growth in resume/experience
- Value diverse backgrounds and non-linear career paths
- Assess problem-solving ability over memorized knowledge

**During Interviews:**
- Ask about how candidates learn and adapt
- Explore their experience with AI tools in depth
- Assess breadth of thinking, not just depth in one area
- Look for evidence of taking on challenges outside comfort zone
- Evaluate cultural fit and collaboration skills

**Trade-offs to Accept:**
- Hire someone with strong backend + willingness to learn frontend vs. waiting for full-stack expert
- Hire someone with cloud experience in AWS who can learn GCP vs. holding out for GCP expert
- Hire someone who has used Claude/GitHub Copilot actively vs. AI-skeptic expert

### Alignment with Org Needs

These principles support:
- **Autonomous Teams:** T-shaped engineers can fill gaps and unblock teammates
- **Cloud Native Ownership:** Engineers with breadth can take on infrastructure alongside product work
- **AI Platform Strategy:** Team members who embrace AI help drive adoption and learning
- **Faster Hiring:** Larger candidate pool, less time waiting for "perfect" match
- **Growth Culture:** Hiring for potential creates a learning-oriented team culture
