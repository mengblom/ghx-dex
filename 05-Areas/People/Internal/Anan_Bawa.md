## Context & Working Style

Anan understands the tension between foundational engineering work and proving business value. He acknowledges that breaking up monoliths and establishing autonomous teams are fundamental practices that shouldn't need elaborate justification, but recognizes the organizational pressure due to historically opaque capacity management.

**Philosophy:**
- "What has to be true" first principles thinking
- Tie work to clear business drivers (exchange stability/uptime)
- Distinguish between different types of work:
  - Customer contractual obligations (Cardinal Health HA/DR)
  - True HA/DR capabilities
  - Foundational engineering improvements

**Pushback areas:**
- Equating uptime with disaster recovery (not the same)
- Excluding critical components (API gateway) from analysis without understanding impact
- Vague terminology ("tech debt", "exchange resiliency") without specific definitions
