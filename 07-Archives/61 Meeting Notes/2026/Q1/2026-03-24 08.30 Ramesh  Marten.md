- Discussion with Ramesh about the Syft team
	- Managed Healthcare Services Inc. was acquired by GHX ~August 2022
	- The solution is called Syft
		- Mobile app, integrated to customer's ERPs, for inventory management
	- The original team was 83 engineers
		- lots of QA - difficult integrations (different ERPs, different Mobile devices etc.)
	- We quickly decided to go to KTLO only
	- 1st layoff: August 2024
	- 2nd layoff: March 2026 (5 more people)
	- 36 people left
	- Reason for layoffs: hard to sell, our sales team did not know how to sell the solution. Perhaps the product was complicated as well
- Discussion about IPA and the new LLM based solution to replace UiPath
	- There is a suggestion to leverage Happiest Minds:
		- To help us meet aggressive timelines
		- Bring LLM expertise that we do not have
			- I am very skeptical about this - I am skeptical that they have much more expertise than we have. If we are going to learn on the job, we should have our FTEs do that, not contractors
	- Background:
		- In December:
			- UiPath contract is expiring
			- First Source contract is expiring
		- After the 3/19 meeting where we presented the LLM based POC, and recommended we go this route instead of switching to BAutomate
			- Everyone (including CJ, Chrystie) agreed we should pursue a homegrown LLM based solution - everyone were excited about the POC
			- CJ asked what we need to ensure success
			- We (Ramesh) said resources/expertise - asked if we could use contractors
			- CJ said yes, but need to stay with Happiest Minds. And he said it needs to be outcome based, not time and material.
	- My position:
		- Again, very skeptical about using Happiest Minds contractors.
		- I suspect Happiest Minds will be scaling the same learning curve as we would on our own
		- We have quite good inhouse expertise / guidance (Eric Arrington, Aaron Srivastava)
		- If it would be a matter of capacity/headcount, we can use contractors somewhere else to free up FTEs to work on this project
		- **The bottom line is - we want to retain the benefit of the learnings, and keep the IP to ourselves, not share it with Happiest Minds.**
