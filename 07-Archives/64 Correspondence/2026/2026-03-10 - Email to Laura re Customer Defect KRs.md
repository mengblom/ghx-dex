*Curtis, Marlin, just an FYI or in case you want to weigh in – otherwise I and Laura will discuss.*

Laura see below. I completely understand Chrystie's concern about demonstrating progress on customer relief - that's legitimate and important. I don't mind using our higher-level OKRs to surface updates on things like customer defect burndown.

But I don't think it makes sense to set specific quarterly KRs targeting specific numbers for Customer Defects in isolation. The reality is that everything we do is in context of everything else. Meaning, there could be a reality where we prioritize exactly zero customer defects for the entire quarter, because a bunch of other things (commercial work, incident remediation, vulnerabilities, Veritas asks etc. etc.) are higher priority. Highly hypothetical, but still. 

Such targets, with such a long time horizon (quarter) kind of goes against how we work – all work, incoming and already on the backlog, is constantly reevaluated and reprioritized as conditions change.

The real KR is having all work visible and properly prioritized (which both you and I have OKRs around). Once that's in place, anyone will be able to see defect burndown alongside everything else and understand the trade-offs we're making. It is also something we can track and report burndown weekly/monthly as part of our regular pod cadence.

For an actual more specific KR, I would be more comfortable with some more classifications in place, something like:
- Reduce ***critical*** defect backlog by 25%
- 95% of ***P1*** defects resolved within 6 weeks

Thoughts?

## Context
This is in response to Chrystie Leonard's request to add a burndown metric to track actual customer defect resolution, not just process establishment. The discussion centers on balancing visibility/accountability with dynamic prioritization principles.
