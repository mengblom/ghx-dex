Hello! Trying to resolve this here, since we are having a hard time finding a meeting time.

In short, I understand the context to be:
- Nate wants a commitment on us delivering and deploying 12 defect remedies in Q2.
- Laura says Product cannot commit to that because they don't know the capacity of the Engineering team.
- Nate is now asking Engineering (me) for that same commitment. 

Here is my perspective: Engineering should not independently commit delivery dates outside of joint Product and Engineering planning. Product is the function that needs to weigh priorities across competing initiatives from different stakeholders. Engineering’s role is to assess feasibility, effort, dependencies, sequencing, and delivery confidence.

We can forecast Q2 at a roadmap level, but we should be careful not to confuse a forecast with a hard commitment. For work that is not yet fully prioritized, refined, and understood, detailed effort estimates and date certainty would be artificial.

In this specific case, committing to delivering the 12 defect remedies in Q2 would require the work to be properly scoped and estimated by Engineering (I don't think this has been done?), and then prioritized highly enough by Product against other competing work, for us to say with real confidence that it will be completed.
